Fees — This Is the Biggest Actual Difference

I'll be blunt: Polymarket charges nothing. Kalshi charges 1-7% per trade. This is the single biggest factor in which platform you should use.

Let me put real numbers on this. In February 2026, I traded about $4,200 in total volume. Here's what I paid:

MetricMy PolymarketMy Kalshi
Monthly volume$2,800$1,400
Fees paid$0.00$47.60
Gas/network fees~$0.30$0.00
Total cost$0.30$47.60

That's $47 a month I'm paying on Kalshi that I wouldn't pay on Polymarket. Over a year? $570. That's real money that goes straight from my potential profit into Kalshi's pocket.

So why do I still use Kalshi? Because sometimes a market only exists on Kalshi, or the Kalshi price is significantly better than Polymarket's. And because I value the regulation for larger positions. But make no mistake — if both platforms have the same market at the same price, I always trade on Polymarket.

My rule of thumb: Use Polymarket as your default. Only go to Kalshi when: (1) the market doesn't exist on Polymarket, (2) the Kalshi price is 3%+ better than Polymarket, or (3) you're making a very large position and want CFTC protection.

Safety & Regulation — The Peace of Mind Factor

If fees favor Polymarket, regulation strongly favors Kalshi.

Kalshi is a CFTC-regulated Designated Contract Market. That means:

  • Customer funds in segregated accounts (your money is protected even if Kalshi goes bankrupt)
  • Regular CFTC audits and compliance checks
  • Federal court backing — a February 2026 ruling confirmed Kalshi's legal status
  • $22 billion valuation — this isn't a fly-by-night operation

Polymarket operates on the Polygon blockchain, which gives a different kind of transparency:

  • Every trade is on-chain — you can verify everything yourself
  • Your funds are in a smart contract, not held by Polymarket directly
  • Partial CFTC oversight, but facing legal challenges in Nevada, Tennessee, Massachusetts
  • Restricted in 33 countries (list growing — Portugal and Hungary were added overnight in Jan 2026)

My take: For positions under $500, I don't worry about it. For larger amounts, I lean toward Kalshi because of the regulatory protection. When I had $8K in open positions during the 2024 election, about 60% was on Kalshi specifically for this reason.

Getting Money In and Out

This is where your personal background determines which platform is easier:

If you're comfortable with crypto → Polymarket is easier

Send USDC on Polygon. Done in 30 seconds. Gas fee: $0.01. I use this method and it's incredibly fast.

If you've never touched crypto → Kalshi is MUCH easier

Link your bank, deposit USD. Same flow as Venmo or Cash App. No blockchain, no wallet addresses, no gas fees. My non-crypto friends unanimously prefer Kalshi for this reason.

Polymarket does have a card on-ramp (you can buy USDC with a credit card), but it charges 2-3% and is clunkier than just linking a bank account. Kalshi's ACH deposits are free.

What Can You Actually Trade?

Both platforms have hundreds of active markets. But the overlap isn't 100%, which is why using both is valuable.

Where Polymarket wins:

  • Crypto markets — "Will Bitcoin hit $X?" type bets. Polymarket has 3-4x more crypto markets than Kalshi.
  • Geopolitics — Wars, elections in non-US countries, UN decisions. Polymarket's global user base creates more demand for these.
  • Culture/Internet — TikTok bans, YouTube milestones, viral events. Kalshi is slower to list these.
  • Sheer volume — More markets are listed, and more niche events get traded.

Where Kalshi wins:

  • Weather — Temperature bets, hurricane tracking, snowfall predictions. Unique to Kalshi.
  • Sports (with legitimacy) — NHL partnership, $1B bracket challenge, more sports-specific markets.
  • Economic data — Fed rate decisions, inflation numbers, GDP. Google Finance integration adds credibility.
  • Mainstream events — Oscars, award shows. Kalshi's CNN/CNBC partnerships drive media coverage.
What I actually trade on each: Crypto and politics on Polymarket (no fees!). Weather and sports on Kalshi (unique markets). Economic data on whichever has the better price.

Day-to-Day Trading: What Does It Actually Feel Like?

This is stuff comparison articles never cover. Let me share what daily use is actually like.

Polymarket's interface

Minimalist, fast, reminds me of a simplified stock trading app. The order book is clean. Prices update in real-time. The X/Twitter integration is genuinely useful — I see Polymarket odds embedded in tweets and can click through to trade. The mobile app is solid but I prefer desktop for serious trading.

Kalshi's interface

More polished, feels like a "real" financial platform. More educational tooltips for beginners. The search is better organized by category. I appreciate the built-in news feed that shows events relevant to your open positions. Mobile app is slightly better than Polymarket's for notifications.

Speed of resolution

Both platforms resolve markets quickly after events conclude. I've never had a dispute with either platform's resolution. Though I've read about occasional controversial resolutions on Polymarket — the decentralized resolution process can sometimes be slower than Kalshi's centralized one.

Questions I Get Asked

Can I transfer money between Polymarket and Kalshi?

Not directly. They're completely separate platforms. To move money from Polymarket to Kalshi, you'd withdraw USDC from Polymarket, sell it for USD on an exchange, deposit USD to Kalshi. Takes a day or two. I keep separate bankrolls on each.

Which has better liquidity?

Kalshi currently has higher weekly volume ($3.4B vs $2.5B), so on the most popular markets, Kalshi's liquidity is often better. But for niche markets, Polymarket sometimes has more trading activity because of its larger global user base. For any market with $100K+ in daily volume, liquidity is fine on both.

Is arbitrage between them actually profitable?

Yes, but the opportunities are getting smaller as both platforms grow. During the 2024 elections I found 5-8% spreads regularly. Now it's more like 1-3% on most events. Still worth it if you're already on both platforms, but don't open accounts solely for arbitrage — the margins are thin.

Do they resolve markets the same way?

Mostly yes, but the process differs. Kalshi resolves centrally — their team decides the outcome based on predefined criteria. Polymarket uses a decentralized resolution through UMA's oracle system, which can occasionally be slower or disputed. In practice, I've never had a different outcome between the two for the same event.
