Why I Added Kalshi After Already Using Polymarket

Three reasons, in order of importance:

1. Arbitrage. The same event can trade at different prices on Kalshi and Polymarket. I've found 3-5% price differences pretty regularly, which is essentially free money if you're on both platforms. During the 2024 election cycle, I found spreads as wide as 8 cents on some markets.

2. Peace of mind. Kalshi is fully CFTC-regulated. Customer funds sit in segregated accounts at a regulated bank. Polymarket is great, but it's a crypto platform with less regulatory oversight. Having money on both means I'm not putting all my eggs in one basket.

3. Some markets are only on Kalshi. Kalshi has weather markets I haven't seen anywhere else — temperature bets, hurricane forecasts, that kind of thing. They also have a strong partnership with the NHL and CNN/CNBC integrations that give them unique sports and economic markets.

What I Genuinely Like About Kalshi

The regulation is real

This isn't marketing fluff. Kalshi is a Designated Contract Market supervised by the CFTC. In February 2026, a federal judge ruled that Kalshi's sports contracts are legally classified as "swaps" under the Commodity Exchange Act — meaning federal law protects them from state gambling bans. That's a huge legal win that no other prediction market has.

I know "just use crypto" sounds easy to crypto natives, but for most people, being able to deposit from their bank account in 2 clicks is the difference between actually signing up and closing the tab. Kalshi nailed this.

The $1 Billion March Madness bracket challenge

What Annoys Me About Kalshi (Honest Negatives)

The fees

This is my biggest gripe. Kalshi charges 1-7% per trade depending on the market. On Polymarket? Zero. If I'm trading $500/week, that's $5-35 in fees on Kalshi vs $0 on Polymarket. Over a year that adds up to real money.

To be fair, Kalshi's fees pay for regulation, customer support, and segregated accounts. You're paying for safety. Whether that's worth it depends on how much you trade.

The referral cap

Kalshi caps your referral earnings at $1,000. That's 40 friends at $25 each. Polymarket has no cap and pays 30% RevShare forever. For anyone building an audience around prediction markets, the Kalshi referral program is disappointing by comparison.

Referral credits aren't real cash

The $25 referral bonus (and the $10 sign-up bonus) are trading credits, not cash. You can't withdraw the bonus directly — only profits you make from trading with it. This isn't a dealbreaker, but it's something you should know. Polymarket's $10 bonus works the same way, to be fair.

Questions People Always Ask Me

Do I need to type in a promo code?

Some partner codes work at sign-up (PREDICTHUB, COVERS, GRINDERS, etc.), but the standard method is clicking a referral link. The link automatically tracks your bonus. If you want to be safe, use both — click a referral link AND enter a code during registration.

Is Kalshi safe? Can I trust them with my money?

Kalshi is the most regulated prediction market platform in the world. It's a CFTC-regulated Designated Contract Market with customer funds in segregated accounts. The company raised a Series E at an $11B valuation (now valued at $22B). I've deposited and withdrawn thousands with zero issues. Is it as trustworthy as Schwab or Fidelity? Getting there. It's certainly more trustworthy than any crypto exchange I've used.

Can I withdraw the $10 bonus?

Not directly. The $10 is trading credit — you use it to trade, and any profits you make from those trades are fully withdrawable. So if you trade the $10 and win $6 in profit, you can withdraw that $6. The original $10 bonus stays as trading credit. Same model as Polymarket.

I already signed up without a code. Can I add one?

You have 72 hours after creating your account to add a referral code, BUT only if you haven't made a deposit yet. If you've already deposited, you're out of luck. I learned this the hard way when I forgot to use a code on my initial sign-up.
