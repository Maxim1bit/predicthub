How Do Prediction Markets Work? Complete Guide

Prediction markets are platforms where you trade on the outcomes of real-world events. Think of them as a stock market for events — instead of buying shares in companies, you buy shares in outcomes like "Will Bitcoin hit $200K?" or "Who will win the next election?"

The Basics: What Are Prediction Markets?

A prediction market creates a contract for a specific event with a binary outcome (Yes/No). Each contract has:

  • Yes shares — pay $1.00 if the event happens
  • No shares — pay $1.00 if the event doesn't happen
  • A price between $0.01 and $0.99 — set by supply and demand

The price of a share directly reflects the market's estimated probability. A "Yes" share priced at $0.70 means the crowd thinks there's a 70% chance the event will happen.

How Prices Work

Prediction market prices are determined by supply and demand, not by a bookmaker. This is the key difference from sports betting.

Key principle: Yes price + No price always equals approximately $1.00. If Yes is trading at $0.65, then No is trading at ~$0.35. This is because one of the two outcomes MUST happen.

Why prices change:

  • New information — A news article, poll result, or announcement shifts people's beliefs
  • Large trades — A whale buying millions of Yes shares pushes the price up
  • Time decay — As the event approaches, prices move closer to $0 or $1
  • Sentiment shifts — Market psychology can cause overreactions

Real Example: How a Trade Works

Market: "Will Bitcoin exceed $200,000 by December 31, 2026?"

Current price: Yes = $0.35 | No = $0.65

Scenario 1: You think yes (BTC will hit $200K)
You buy 100 Yes shares at $0.35 each = $35 total cost
If Bitcoin hits $200K → shares pay $1.00 each → you receive $100 → profit: $65 (186% return)
If Bitcoin doesn't hit $200K → shares pay $0 → loss: $35

Scenario 2: You think no (BTC won't hit $200K)
You buy 100 No shares at $0.65 each = $65 total cost
If Bitcoin doesn't hit $200K → shares pay $1.00 each → you receive $100 → profit: $35 (54% return)
If Bitcoin hits $200K → shares pay $0 → loss: $65

Scenario 3: Sell early for profit
You bought Yes at $0.35. Next week, big news pushes Yes to $0.55.
You sell 100 shares at $0.55 = $55 → profit: $20 (57% return) without waiting for the event

  3. Browse markets — Find events you understand and have an opinion on

Basic Strategies for Beginners

1. News Trading

React to breaking news faster than the market. If a politician drops out of a race and you see the news first, you can buy/sell before the market fully adjusts.

2. Fade the Crowd

When the market overreacts to news (emotion-driven moves), bet against the crowd. Prices often revert after the initial panic or euphoria settles.

3. Arbitrage

If the same event has different prices on Polymarket and Kalshi, buy the cheaper side and sell the expensive side for risk-free profit.

4. Portfolio Approach

Spread your capital across multiple markets. Even if some positions lose, your winners can more than compensate. Think of it like a diversified investment portfolio.

Risk Warning: Prediction markets involve real money and real risk. You can lose your entire investment on a contract. Never trade more than you can afford to lose. Start small ($1-5 per trade) until you understand how markets move.
