How Do Prediction Markets Work?

Prediction markets let you trade on the outcome of real-world events. Unlike sportsbooks, prices are set by supply and demand — not by a bookmaker. You buy shares of "Yes" or "No" at a price between $0.01 and $1.00. If you're right, your shares pay out $1.00 each.

Example: "Will Bitcoin hit $200K by December 2026?" If "Yes" shares trade at $0.35, the market implies a 35% probability. Buy at $0.35, and if BTC hits $200K, you earn $0.65 per share (186% return). If it doesn't, you lose your $0.35 per share.

Key advantages over traditional betting:

  • No vig/juice — Polymarket charges zero fees; you trade peer-to-peer
  • Real-time pricing — Prices update instantly based on new information
  • Diverse markets — Politics, economics, tech, culture, weather, and more
  • Partial exit — Sell your position anytime before the event resolves

Can I make money on prediction markets?

Yes, many traders profit by accurately predicting event outcomes or by trading price movements. However, prediction markets carry risk — you can lose your entire investment on a contract. Start small and learn how markets work before trading larger amounts.
